British English has 44 phonetic sounds

Monophthong Sounds

They are made with a single position of the mouth (lips, tongue, jaw). There are 13 monophthong sounds.

Diphthong

They are made by moving from one position to another in the same sound. There are 7 Diphthong sounds.

Fricative Sounds

They are made by squeezing air through a block in the mouth or throat. There are 9 Fricative sounds.

Plosive Sounds

They are made by fully blocking the air and releasing it in an explosion. There are 7 Plosive Sounds.

Affricate Sounds

They are made by beginning as plosive (block of air) and then release as a fricative (squeeze of air). There are 2 affricate sounds.

Approximant Sounds

They are made like vowels without fully blocking the air as it leaves the body. There are 4 Aproximant sounds.

Nasal Sounds

They are made by releasing some sound through the nose. There are 3 nasal sounds.
